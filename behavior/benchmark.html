BEHAVIOR-1K · benchmark episodes

Egocentric episodes

Real rollouts in BEHAVIOR-1K v3.7.2 (OmniGibson + Isaac Sim), rendered headless on 8×H200. Each is a Tiago robot executing a chain of semantic action primitives; we render the robot's first-person camera and composite the action scene graph along the bottom (current action highlighted). Episodes are chosen to span the interaction families — including ones a symbolic simulator cannot do (articulated open/close, and a physically-simulated cut).

Slice / cut ★ rich

“Pick up the knife and slice the apple into pieces.”

hardness 2transition ruleSliceable → slices
GRASP(knife) → CUT(apple)  — fires the SlicingRule
Why these four: they walk up BEHAVIOR's interaction ladder — a plain kinematic pick-and-place, an articulated open/place-inside/close, a stateful toggle, and a cut that triggers a physical transition rule and spawns apple slices. The last two have no equivalent in a symbolic simulator like VirtualHome.
Method

How each episode is made

StepDetail
Scene + robotInteractive scene Rs_int loaded with a Tiago bimanual mobile manipulator and the task objects.
PolicySymbolicSemanticActionPrimitives — the robot navigates its base to each target and executes the primitive; object states update through BEHAVIOR's state machine & transition rules.
CameraA first-person camera mounted at the robot's head, aimed at the active target each frame (HQ RTX rendering: reflections, ambient occlusion, DLSS denoise).
Action graphThe chain of primitives is drawn as a node-edge graph at the bottom; the current edge is highlighted green as the episode plays. Hardness = number of edges.
RenderHeadless on an 8×H200 cluster (Isaac Sim RTX renderer via Vulkan), one episode per GPU.
The same hardness scheme (edges in the action scene graph) and easy/hard intuition are used on the VirtualHome benchmark, so the two simulators are directly comparable — BEHAVIOR adds the physically-grounded actions on top.
